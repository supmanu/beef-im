## Direction 1: Smoke & Ledger

**One-line mood:** The calm after the grill dies down — charcoal silence, one amber ember still alive, and a leather ledger open on the table.

**Palette (5 colors with hex):**
- Primary background: #141210 (coffee-charcoal)
- Surface / card: #1E1C19 (warm dark)
- Body text: #D8D0C4 (parchment-bone)
- Accent (limited use): #C87941 (ember copper)
- Critical signal (errors / highlights): #E8533F (cherry-red glow)

**Typography stack:**
- Display headline (Thai + Latin pairing): Chonburi + Playfair Display — slab-serifs give editorial weight in both scripts; Chonburi's Thai slab letterforms echo Playfair's ink-traps naturally
- Body (Thai + Latin pairing): Sarabun + Source Serif 4 — warm, readable serif body for long-form reading on both topics without favoring either
- Mono / data / accent: IBM Plex Mono — financial table data reads cold against the warm surroundings, deliberate contrast

**Visual motif:** **Ember trace** — a thin, diagonal copper-amber line (1px, #C87941) that travels across each page section like a heat line on thermal paper. It connects insurance teardowns to recipe sections without switching visual language. On article pages it runs under the headline then fades; on the homepage it weaves through all content blocks as a continuous thread.

**Animation/interaction signature:** **Ember flicker on content reveal** — when a card or section enters the viewport, a 0.6s animation plays: the content fades in (opacity 0→1) while a single 2px horizontal ember line (#C87941) fires across the top of the element from left to right at 300px/s, then dissolves. The line uses `clip-path` animation, never blocks content, and is replaced by `opacity` transition on `prefers-reduced-motion`. One line, one direction, every time.

**Layout instinct:** Long-scroll cinematic narrative. Eye lands on a single bold statement per viewport, then scrolls into detail. Hero = one headline, one ember line, one supporting line of text. Dense insurance content expands below the fold — no competing columns. Feels like scrolling through a film sequence.

**3 reference points:**
1. The menu typography of Le Bernardin's website, 2024 — restraint in the steakhouse register
2. The Korean drama *Misaeng* (2018) opening title sequence — charcoal palette, copper serif type, one ember element
3. Rayce Media's "Oppenheimer" web experience, 2023 — filmic dark with selective warmth highlighting data

**Why it fits beef.im:** The dark canvas treats insurance forensics and cooking content with equal visual seriousness — one ember thread unites them. Fire-as-data isn't literal grill imagery; it's the line between raw and done, the margin between risk and payout.

## Direction 2: Broadside

**One-line mood:** The authoritative daily broadsheet that decided finance teardowns and grill recipes deserve the same typeset gravity — no hierarchy games, just ink on paper.

**Palette (5 colors with hex):**
- Primary background: #F5F0E8 (unbleached newsprint)
- Surface / card: #FFFFFF (white space, edited)
- Body text: #1A1A1A (near-black ink)
- Accent (limited use): #B8860B (old-gold dateline)
- Critical signal (errors / highlights): #C41E3A (red-ink revision)

**Typography stack:**
- Display headline (Thai + Latin pairing): Mitr + Oswald — both condensed verticals with punch; Mitr's Thai tall-x-height matches Oswald's compressed Latin perfectly for stacked headlines
- Body (Thai + Latin pairing): Sarabun + Georgia Pro — reliable serif body text with real Thai optical sizing; reads like a paper
- Mono / data / accent: Courier Prime — deliberate typewriter anachronism for insurance clause numbers, premiums, cooking temperatures

**Visual motif:** **The rule** — thick horizontal rules (3px, #1A1A1A) section every content block like a broadsheet story divider. The rule is never decorative; it always carries semantic weight (section break, topic shift, data boundary). Below the rule = new subject. Between insurance analysis and recipe content sits one thick rule, then both continue with identical type treatment. No visual hierarchy prejudice.

**Animation/interaction signature:** **Ink-set stamp reveal on card hover** — on hover, content cards shift down 3px while a flat 1px #1A1A1A bottom-border draws in from left to right at 200ms, mimicking a printing press stamp settling onto paper. Shadow stays flat (no elevation change). On mobile tap, the border appears on `:active` and holds for 400ms before releasing. Zero transform complexity, GPU-free.

**Layout instinct:** Magazine-grid with newspaper density. Above the fold: one lead story (2/3 width) + sidebar stack (1/3). Below: 3-column grid collapses to single-column scroll on mobile. Insurance teardowns and recipes sit side-by-side with identical card format — the reader chooses by headline, not by visual genre.

**3 reference points:**
1. The Verge's "Verge 5" redesign, 2024 — editorial grid that respects both long-form and short-form equally
2. Monocle magazine Issue 170, "The Entrepreneurs" — broadsheet layout with gold dateline accents
3. The New York Times' Morning Briefing newsletter layout, 2024 — rule-divided content density with breathing room

**Why it fits beef.im:** *ดูเนื้อ ไม่ดูหน้า* is literally "judge by substance, not appearance" — this direction gives insurance and cooking identical visual weight and forces merit to win. The broadsheet is the most credible format in Thai readers' experience; it says "this matters" without screaming.

## Direction 3: Certified

**One-line mood:** Every piece of content arrives stamped, graded, and sealed — like a USDA inspection certificate that decided to also grade your financial literacy.

**Palette (5 colors with hex):**
- Primary background: #FAFAF8 (clinical bright)
- Surface / card: #FFFFFF (clean slate)
- Body text: #2D2D2D (soft ink)
- Accent (limited use): #1B4D3E (inspector green — the green of grading stamps)
- Critical signal (errors / highlights): #D4380D (grade-fail red)

**Typography stack:**
- Display headline (Thai + Latin pairing): Kanit (weight 700–900) + DM Serif Display — Kanit's geometric Thai carries the certification authority; DM Serif Display gives Latin the serif gravitas of official documents
- Body (Thai + Latin pairing): Noto Sans Thai + Inter — clean, clinical, high-legibility for long insurance teardowns; no personality, all function
- Mono / data / accent: JetBrains Mono — premium data tables and code-like insurance clause references

**Visual motif:** **The grade stamp** — a circular or elliptical stamp element (2px border, #1B4D3E, slightly rotated -2°) appears on every content piece. Insurance articles get grade stamps like "A+ ผลประกัน" or "B- ความคุ้มค่า"; recipes get "Medium-Rare ✓" or "Well-Done ✗" doneness ratings. The stamp is never decorative — it IS the content's verdict. Even the homepage hero has one large stamp: "เนื้อๆ" rotated slightly.

**Animation/interaction signature:** **Stamp thud on section entry** — when a content card enters the viewport (Intersection Observer, 0.2 threshold), a grade stamp element (40×40px circle, 2px border) scales from 1.3× to 1.0× with an ease-out bounce over 300ms, accompanied by a 0.4 opacity `box-shadow` that expands from 0 to 4px then collapses, simulating a rubber stamp hitting paper. The stamp content itself has a 150ms text-fill delay from the scale start, so the text appears to "ink in" as the stamp lands. Reduced-motion: opacity 0→1, no scale.

**Layout instinct:** Asymmetric editorial — large stamp + headline anchor the left 2/3; supporting metadata (date, read time, category) stacked right 1/3. Article pages: stamp in top-left corner stays fixed as reader scrolls, always grading this article. Feels like opening a certified document.

**3 reference points:**
1. The USDA meat grading shield (updated 2023 visual standards) — the circular stamp motif, authority without decoration
2. Notion's certification & verification badges, 2024 — institutional confidence in a clean interface
3. Aesop's product packaging and website, 2025 — clinical-precision typography with organic product tension

**Why it fits beef.im:** Grading is the conceptual bridge — meat gets graded (Prime, Choice, Select), insurance gets graded (A+, B-). The stamp motif unifies both content domains under one visual judgment system. The user reads "เนื้อๆ" and sees certification, not decoration.

## Direction 4: Hearth Cut

**One-line mood:** A private omakase counter at 9PM — ten seats, one focus, complete silence except for the cut and the sizzle.

**Palette (5 colors with hex):**
- Primary background: #0A0A0A (void black)
- Surface / card: #161616 (near-black)
- Body text: #E8E4DE (warm parchment)
- Accent (limited use): #A0845C (aged washi / tannin gold)
- Critical signal (errors / highlights): #E84545 (hibachi flash-red)

**Typography stack:**
- Display headline (Thai + Latin pairing): Bai Jamjuree (weight 700) + Space Grotesk — Bai Jamjuree's geometric Thai has the architectural restraint of Japanese signage; Space Grotesk matches its x-height and neutrality without competing
- Body (Thai + Latin pairing): IBM Plex Sans Thai + IBM Plex Sans — technical-precision body that refuses to be warm; the cold body text against the warm palette creates productive tension
- Mono / data / accent: SF Mono / Fira Code — precise data rendering in tables and insurance clause numbers

**Visual motif:** **The single mark** — every page, every section has exactly one deliberate mark: a thin (1px) horizontal line in #A0845C that appears once and once only. No doubling, no decoration. On the homepage it sits between the hero statement and the content grid. On articles it appears under the byline. On recipe pages it separates ingredients from method. The mark is the slash of the knife — decisive, singular, done.

**Animation/interaction signature:** **Sear reveal on scroll** — text and card elements use `clip-path: inset(0 100% 0 0)` that animates to `inset(0 0% 0 0)` on scroll-entry over 800ms with `cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.1, 0.25, 1)`. Content appears to sear in from left to right, like a torch passing over protein. Only primary content blocks (headlines, first paragraph, hero card) get the sear; secondary elements (sidebar, tags, related links) use simple opacity 0→1 at 200ms delay. Mobile: same timing, but `clip-path` direction switches to bottom-to-top (vertical scroll context). Reduced-motion: instant opacity.

**Layout instinct:** One-screen statement per section. Homepage: single centered headline, one mark, then a vertical stack of cards with generous negative space. No grid columns competing. On mobile: even more whitespace — the emptiness IS the design. Feels like looking at one plate at a time on a black counter.

**3 reference points:**
1. Noma's website (Copenhagen), 2024 — extreme restraint, black void, single focal element per viewport
2. Dieter Rams' Braun ET66 calculator interface — reduction to the essential mark
3. Sushi Saito's reservation page (Tokyo, 2023 archive) — nothing extraneous, one path forward

**Why it fits beef.im:** Omakase restraint treats insurance analysis and cooking with the same hushed seriousness — no visual noise means substance must carry every pixel. The "single mark" is the visual *เนื้อๆ*: one cut, one point, no excess. The dark palette demands the content prove itself or leave.

## Direction 5: Temperature Grade

**One-line mood:** What if budget spreadsheets and doneness charts shared the same heat-map — blue for safe, orange for caution, red for critical, and every content piece lives somewhere on that spectrum?

**Palette (5 colors with hex):**
- Primary background: #F7F7F5 (warm off-white)
- Surface / card: #FFFFFF (pure white card)
- Body text: #1C1C1E (near-black)
- Accent (limited use): #3B7DD8 (data-blue — cool, trustworthy, limited to interactive elements and links)
- Critical signal (errors / highlights): #E8482F (danger-red — used for urgent insurance warnings AND well-done doneness indicators)

**Typography stack:**
- Display headline (Thai + Latin pairing): Anuphan (weight 700–800) + Outfit — Anuphan's Thai has friendly but confident proportions; Outfit matches its geometric skeleton while staying legible at display sizes
- Body (Thai + Latin pairing): Sarabun + Source Sans 3 — maximum readability pair for dense insurance content, proven at small sizes on mobile
- Mono / data / accent: Tabular Nums in Inter Tight + JetBrains Mono for explicit code — insurance tables need tabular figures above all

**Visual motif:** **The temperature bar** — a thin (4px tall) horizontal gradient bar that sits at the top of every content card and article page. Insurance content: gradient runs from #3B7DD8 (safe/blue) through #F5A623 (caution/amber) to #E8482F (critical/red) — representing risk level. Cooking content: same gradient but mapped to doneness (rare/blue → medium/amber → well-done/red). The bar IS the content preview — you can scan the homepage and "read" the temperature of every article before clicking.

**Animation/interaction signature:** **Gradient shift on hover** — on desktop hover (or mobile tap), the temperature bar expands from 4px to 24px height over 300ms using `transition: height`, revealing the gradient at larger scale. Simultaneously, a small tooltip appears above the bar showing the exact grade label (e.g., "ความเสี่ยง: ปานกลาง" or "ระดับความสุก: Medium-Rare") with a 150ms fade-in. The expansion uses `will-change: height` to stay GPU-composited. On mouse-leave, bar collapses back in 200ms. Reduced-motion: tooltip only, no height change.

**Layout instinct:** Magazine grid with temperature scanning. Homepage: masonry/waterfall layout where cards are organized by temperature (hottest/most urgent left-top, cooling right-bottom). Eye scans left-to-right reading a heat narrative. Insurance alerts and new recipes both occupy the "hot" zone — no genre prejudice, only temperature.

**3 reference points:**
1. Bloomberg Terminal data heat maps, 2024 — financial data as color-temperature, instantly scannable
2. Serious Eats' "Food Lab" doneness gradient charts — thermal imagery applied to cooking science
3. Vercel's deployment status dashboard (2024–25) — neutral surface with selective color signaling

**Why it fits beef.im:** Temperature is the universal language connecting insurance risk assessment (danger levels) with cooking (doneness levels). The heat-map isn't a metaphor — it's a shared data visualization language that makes both content types legible at a glance. This is *เนื้อๆ* as information architecture.
