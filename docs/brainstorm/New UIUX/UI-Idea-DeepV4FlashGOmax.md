# Brand UI/UX Directions — ประกันเนื้อๆ (beef.im)

> **Brief:** Fresh from scratch. 5 directions + 1 wildcard. No expedition theme, no synthwave, no blood-and-cleaver. Thai typography considered throughout.

---

## Direction 1: Slaughterhouse Chic

**One-line mood:** Clinical precision meets warm-blooded craft — a butcher's counter under museum lighting.

**Palette (5 colors with hex):**
- Primary background: `#0D0D0D` (near-black — abattoir darkness)
- Surface / card: `#1A1A1A` (dark marble)
- Body text: `#E8E0D8` (warm bone)
- Accent (limited use): `#C73B2A` (fresh-cut crimson — used only for data callouts and critical info)
- Critical signal (errors / highlights): `#E8A317` (fat-gold — warnings and highlights)

**Typography stack:**
- Display headline: **Prompt Black** + **Fraunces** (super-wide italic alternates)
- Body: **IBM Plex Sans Thai Looped** + **Inter**
- Mono / data / accent: **JetBrains Mono** (for premium contract numbers, data points)

**Visual motif:** The "cut line" — a razor-thin 1px crimson line that appears diagonally across full-bleed hero images, section dividers, and card backgrounds, suggesting the knife's edge between risk and reward.

**Animation/interaction signature:** "Blood-drip data reveal" — on viewport entry, numerical values (premiums, percentages, stats) fill from bottom to top as if a liquid is rising inside the digit's outline, then settle with a subtle overflow ripple at the top of each glyph.

**Layout instinct:** Asymmetric magazine — two-thirds text, one-third pulled quotes / data callouts. Long-scroll narrative with brutalist section markers. Mobile: single-column with staggered reveals.

**3 reference points:**
- Moooi Random Chandelier (bones as light — elegant macabre)
- Tomas Maier's Bottega Veneta stores (raw concrete + surgical precision + warmth)
- The Butcher's Daughter NYC (restaurant brand that made a slaughterhouse feel like a wine bar)

**Why it fits beef.im:** The visual tension between near-black brutality and warm bone text mirrors the site's core paradox — forensic financial analysis and primal grill culture living in the same space without apology.

## Direction 2: The Dossier

**One-line mood:** You've been handed a classified folder on your own money — redacted, urgent, and impossible to ignore.

**Palette (5 colors with hex):**
- Primary background: `#F5F0E8` (aged document stock)
- Surface / card: `#FFFFFF` (interrogation-room white)
- Body text: `#1A1A1A` (typewriter ink)
- Accent (limited use): `#D6382B` (redacted-stamp red)
- Critical signal (errors / highlights): `#2B5C8A` (bureaucratic blue — used for hyperlinks, corrections)

**Typography stack:**
- Display headline: **Sarabun Bold** + **EB Garamond** (all small caps, widely tracked)
- Body: **Anuphan** + **Source Serif**
- Mono / data / accent: **IBM Plex Mono** (typewriter weight)

**Visual motif:** The redaction bar — solid black rectangles of varying widths that obscure fragments of text on hero sections and card previews, implying there is always more beneath the surface. Revealed on hover/scroll.

**Animation/interaction signature:** "Stamp-activation" — every CTA, filter toggle, and primary interaction triggers a 200ms rubber-stamp impact animation: the element compresses vertically, a subtle *thump* vibration on the card, then an ink-bleed spread outward from the impact point that fades in 600ms.

**Layout instinct:** Dense dossier layout — stacked evidence (data tables, contract excerpts, annotated screenshots) with wide margins for handwritten-style annotations. Desktop: two-column evidence + marginalia. Mobile: accordion dossier sections.

**3 reference points:**
- *Zodiac* (2007) title sequence — typewriter, redacted documents, obsessive annotation
- *The Report* (2019) — torture-report aesthetic of pure information, no ornament
- The Intercept's document-explorer interface (redacted PDF viewer with reveal-on-hover)

**Why it fits beef.im:** Insurance contracts and financial terms are literally dossiers that people need decoded — this direction treats every article like an investigative briefing, making the reader feel like an insider rather than a customer.

## Direction 3: Ember

**One-line mood:** The glow of dying coals at golden hour — warm, patient, just hot enough to sear.

**Palette (5 colors with hex):**
- Primary background: `#1C1510` (charred ember)
- Surface / card: `#2A2119` (ash bed)
- Body text: `#E8D5C4` (glow-light)
- Accent (limited use): `#FF6B35` (ember core — used sparingly for active states and data)
- Critical signal (errors / highlights): `#4AE0D0` (smoke-teal — a cooling counterpoint, used for corrections)

**Typography stack:**
- Display headline: **Bai Jamjuree SemiBold** + **Playfair Display** (italic swashes for recipe headers)
- Body: **Prompt Light** + **Proxima Nova** (or **Inter**)
- Mono / data / accent: **DM Mono** (slightly irregular, like hand-poured numbers)

**Visual motif:** Light-through-smoke — every image, card, and data visualization is overlaid with a faint, undulating warm-gradient wash (gold → amber → charcoal) that suggests reading a menu by firelight. Not a literal filter — a subtle color-field vignette that shifts with scroll position.

**Animation/interaction signature:** "Sear-line glow" — on hover, a thin horizontal line at 30% from the top of any interactive card heats from amber to white-hot over 400ms, then cools back to amber over 800ms on mouse leave, leaving a 1-second ghost gradient that fades. Emulates the grill-sear mark across a steak.

**Layout instinct:** Cinematic letterbox — 21:9 aspect ratio enforced for hero sections and recipe headers regardless of device. Below the fold: single-column long-scroll with alternating full-screen data cards and horizontally-scrolling recipe galleries. Mobile: collapses to vertical stack, but keeps letterbox ratio on hero.

**3 reference points:**
- *The Bear* (Hulu) — kitchen heat, close-cropped texture, amber-gelled lighting
- *Noma* restaurant website (noma.dk) — elemental, fire-centered, material honesty
- *Chernobyl* (HBO) — the golden incandescence of danger and heat as a design material

**Why it fits beef.im:** Ember's warmth softens the hard edges of insurance content without diluting authority — the firelight makes premium numbers feel grounded and recipes feel sacred, bridging both halves of the brand with a single elemental language.

## Direction 4: Tabloid

**One-line mood:** Thai street-food energy meets Bloomberg Terminal — loud, dense, and impossible to ignore.

**Palette (5 colors with hex):**
- Primary background: `#F7F3E8` (newsprint)
- Surface / card: `#FFFFFF` (flash white)
- Body text: `#111111` (headline black)
- Accent (limited use): `#FFD600` (taxi-yellow — headlines, breaking news ribbons)
- Critical signal (errors / highlights): `#E1251B` (news-stand red — price drops, warnings, scandals)

**Typography stack:**
- Display headline: **Chakra Petch Bold** (sharp Thai terminals + tight tracking) + **Titillium Web Black**
- Body: **Sarabun Regular** + **Roboto Slab**
- Mono / data / accent: **JetBrains Mono** (for ticker-style price streams)

**Visual motif:** "The ticker" — a persistent horizontal scrolling strip at page bottom (desktop) or below the header (mobile) that cycles the latest insurance rate changes, health-stat headlines, and recipe tips in a single, non-stop stream. 1.5rem caps, monospace, on a yellow background.

**Animation/interaction signature:** "Pull-quote explode" — tapping/clicking a highlighted statistic or pull quote triggers a 300ms burst where the text expands to fill the viewport (center-anchored scale to 4x), the background behind it flashes yellow, then the full article section opens below. One gesture, zero clicks-through-menu.

**Layout instinct:** New York Post / Khaosod front page density — stacked decks of headlines, sub-headlines, data nuggets, and inset images. No whitespace wasted. Grid: 6-column on desktop, 3 on tablet, 2 on mobile. Every pixel earns its place.

**3 reference points:**
- Bloomberg Terminal splash screen (neon on black, data density, money-as-spectacle)
- *Thai Rath* print edition (the ultimate Thai tabloid — chaotic hierarchy, bold color blocks)
- Khoi Vinh's *Subtraction.com* redesign (2017) — newsprint beauty in a responsive grid

**Why it fits beef.im:** Insurance and finance are treated as boring in Thailand — this direction makes them feel urgent, breaking, and vital. The tabloid register gives recipe content equal weight: today's hottest dish and today's most critical contract trap, side by side, both demanding attention.

## Direction 5: Immersion Tank

**One-line mood:** Reading inside a sensory deprivation chamber where only the numbers and the fire are real.

**Palette (5 colors with hex):**
- Primary background: `#000000` (absolute black — OLED true black)
- Surface / card: `#111111` (one step from void)
- Body text: `#F0F0E6` (warm white, 85% opacity)
- Accent (limited use): `#FF3B30` (pulse red — only for data that demands action)
- Critical signal (errors / highlights): `#30D158` (hospital-monitor green — corrections, confirmations, safe zones)

**Typography stack:**
- Display headline: **Anuphan ExtraBold** + **FK Screamer** (or **Bebas Neue** — ultra-condensed display)
- Body: **Noto Sans Thai** + **Inter** (Medium weight, 0.01em letter-spacing)
- Mono / data / accent: **SF Mono** or **JetBrains Mono** (at 14px with 1.8 line-height)

**Visual motif:** "The void halo" — every content block (text, image, card, table) floats on the black void with a 1px soft-luminosity edge that glows subtly (5px blur, 10% opacity of the accent color). Nothing touches the edges; everything is an island in dark space. Creates the feeling of data suspended in zero gravity.

**Animation/interaction signature:** "Pulse-to-focus" — on page load, the first content block (hero) starts with a slow 4s breath-pulse (scale 1.0 → 1.02 → 1.0, opacity 100% → 85% → 100%). As the user scrolls, the pulse transfers to whatever block is most in-viewport. The entire page breathes. Below the fold, inactive blocks rest at 60% opacity and only come to full opacity + pulse when centered.

**Layout instinct:** Single-object focus — one card, one section, one data point at a time. Generous black breathing room. Horizontal scroll for comparative data (contract A vs. contract B), vertical scroll for narrative. Mobile: identical, but cards stack tighter. Every page is a sequence of reveals, not a wall.

**3 reference points:**
- Apple Vision Pro UI (floating panels, depth, material lightness in void)
- *2049: Blade Runner* (opening sequence — data fragments in black space, warm orange in black)
- *House of Cards* title sequence (time-lapse in black, but replace DC with Bangkok at night)

**Why it fits beef.im:** The black void neutralizes the genre clash — both insurance analysis and recipe content exist as floating content islands with equal weight. The breathing pulse makes static contract language feel alive and the fire of recipe photography feel contained. Substance-only, no decoration.

## Top Pick + Why

**Ship Direction 3: Ember.** It's the only direction that metabolizes both halves of the paradox into a single atmospheric language — the warm glow works for a golden-hour grill photo AND for a retirement-fund stress test. The trade-off: you lose the "urgent insider" energy that Tabloid offers, and you need exceptional photography to carry the atmospheric load (stock imagery will murder this direction). But if the photography is good, Ember is the most ownable and the hardest for competitors to copy — no one in Thai finance is building a brand around firelight.

## [WILDCARD] Direction 6: Garment Label

No palette. No type stack. Every visit, the site randomly assigns one of 20+ "season" palettes (SS26, FW26, Pre-Fall, etc.) drawn from actual fashion-house color cycles — one visit it's Bottega Veneta green-on-green, next visit it's Margiela all-white-on-white. The visual motif: a woven-label stamp in the top-left corner of every page that reads "BEEF.IM / EST. 2025 / MADE IN THAILAND" in a tiny serif, exactly like a garment care tag. All photography is cropped to look like lookbook spreads — headless, contextless, high-contrast. The animation signature: a slow cloth-drape physics simulation on the nav drawer, as if the menu is a silk scarf being laid across the screen. It would never ship because seasonal palette rotation destroys content consistency and confuses mobile-retention patterns, but it would generate insane social-media buzz from design Twitter, Thai fashion accounts, and anyone who believes a finance platform should never look like Margiela.
