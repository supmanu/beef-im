## 🌉 THE ANALOGY LIBRARY

### **CATEGORY A: COST & FEE MECHANISMS (#001-#010)**

---

#### **#001: The BMW X5 Slope** (Asset Allocation)
- **Analogy:** BMW X5 climbing 35° slope distributes torque; if one wheel slips, power transfers to wheels with traction
- **Finance:** Retirement is a slippery slope. You need Traction (Stability), not Speed (Returns). 100% in one asset = one wheel on mud
- **Verdict:** "Check your portfolio's traction. If 100% in one asset class, shift to 4WD immediately."

---

#### **#002: Churchill's Skirt** (Policy Review)
- **Analogy:** "Long enough to cover the subject, short enough to create interest"
- **Finance:** Most ignore 50-page policies. Audit for two things: Does it cover essential risks? Is the summary simple?
- **Verdict:** "If your agent gives 50 pages without 1-page summary, reject it. Demand the 'Skirt Summary'."

---

#### **#003: The Comparative Scale** (Inflation)
- **Analogy:** 500 Billion floats meaninglessly until anchored to Thailand's GDP
- **Finance:** 10M Baht today = Thong Lo condo. In 20 years with 3% inflation = Bang Na studio. Anchor goals to real assets
- **Verdict:** "Don't calculate the Number. Calculate the Purchasing Power."

---

#### **#004: The Conductor** (Estate Planning)
- **Analogy:** Conductor doesn't play—controls tempo and entry of every expert player
- **Finance:** Wealthy mindset = Conductor, not violin player. Know when to signal Lawyer, Accountant
- **Verdict:** "Stop trying to play every instrument. Step up to the podium. Hire the players."

---

#### **#005: The Car Engine** (Unit-Linked COI)
- **Analogy:** Two cars, same fuel tank. One engine burns fuel while idling (COI)
- **Finance:** Premium = fuel in. COI = fuel burned to idle. At older age, engine becomes gas guzzler
- **Verdict:** "Ask to see the Burn Rate (COI Table), not just the Fuel Tank (Premium)."

---

#### **#006: The Termite Colony** (Inflation)
- **Analogy:** House looks perfect outside, but termites eat support beams 24/7 until floor collapses
- **Finance:** Inflation isn't a storm—it's termites. Money looks safe in bank, purchasing power eaten at 3%/year
- **Verdict:** "Inspect your walls. Calculate what savings will actually buy in 20 years."

---

#### **#007: The Gym Membership** (Policy Utilization)
- **Analogy:** Expensive gym membership ≠ fitness. Using equipment = fitness
- **Finance:** Unit-Linked = black card gym. Paying premium without managing fund allocation = donating to gym owner
- **Verdict:** "Check your utilization rate. When did you last review fund allocation?"

---

#### **#008: The Sherpa** (Agent Incentives)
- **Analogy:** Sherpa paid per day. Does he want you to reach summit fast or take scenic route?
- **Finance:** Agent gets 40% of premium only in Year 1. No incentive to help in Year 10
- **Verdict:** "Ask your Sherpa: 'How are you paid?' If he can't answer clearly, find a new guide."

---

#### **#009: The Boiling Frog** (COI Creep)
- **Analogy:** Frog in boiling water jumps out. Frog in cold water heated 1°C/minute cooks to death
- **Finance:** 30% fee hike = jump out. COI rising 5%/year = feels fine until age 60 when cash value is cooked
- **Verdict:** "Check the temperature. Request COI table and plot the 20-year trend."

---

#### **#010: The Buffet Strategy** (Over-diversification)
- **Analogy:** Loading plate with sushi, pizza, curry isn't balanced diet—it's stomach ache
- **Finance:** 20 different funds isn't diversification. True diversification = 5-course meal, each dish has specific job
- **Verdict:** "Count your funds. If more than 7, ask: What job does each one do?"

---

### **CATEGORY B: TIMING & DECISION POINTS (#011-#017)**

---

#### **#011: The Condo Pre-Sale** (Surrender Value) ⭐
- **Analogy:** Pay 500K down payment on condo. Cancel after 6 months = forfeit down payment. Wait until transfer = 3M asset
- **Finance:** Surrendering early = canceling condo after down payment. Years 1-3 surrender value often close to zero
- **Verdict:** "ก่อนเวนคืน ให้คำนวณว่าเหลืออีกกี่ปีจึงจะถึงจุดคุ้มทุน บางทีการถือต่อถูกกว่าการเริ่มใหม่"

---

#### **#012: The Quicksand Loan** (Policy Loan)
- **Analogy:** Quicksand looks solid until you step on it. Struggling makes you sink faster. Standing still also sinks
- **Finance:** Policy loan charges 6-8%, cash value earns 3-4%. Difference = sinking 2-4%/year
- **Verdict:** "If you have policy loan, calculate sinking rate. Repay or surrender—don't stand still."

---

#### **#013: The Tax Refund ATM** (SSF/RMF)
- **Analogy:** ATM gives cash today but debits account in 30 years—with interest
- **Finance:** SSF/RMF: Put 200K, get 70K back. But locked 10 years (SSF) or until 55 (RMF). Early withdrawal = return 70K + penalty + tax
- **Verdict:** "Before using Tax ATM, ask: Can I lock this money? If not, skip the refund."

---

#### **#014: The Paused Treadmill** (Premium Holiday)
- **Analogy:** Pause treadmill but timer keeps running. Resume = less time left than expected
- **Finance:** Premium Holiday = paused treadmill. COI still deducts monthly from cash value
- **Verdict:** "If on Premium Holiday, check monthly COI deduction. Calculate months until cash value = zero."

---

#### **#015: The Photoshopped Brochure** (Illustrated Returns)
- **Analogy:** Dating profile heavily edited. Real person looks nothing like picture
- **Finance:** 6% projected return = photoshopped. Guaranteed column = real person you'll meet in 20 years
- **Verdict:** "Ignore Illustrated column. Ask: 'What is GUARANTEED value at Year 20?'"

---

#### **#016: The Wrong Address Package** (Beneficiary)
- **Analogy:** Order 50K iPhone, type wrong address. Package arrives—at someone else's door
- **Finance:** Policy = package. Beneficiary = delivery address. "ทายาทตามกฎหมาย" = probate court. Ex-wife from 10 years ago = her
- **Verdict:** "Pull out every policy. Check beneficiary. Update TODAY if wrong address."

---

#### **#017: The Quarantine Period** (Waiting Period)
- **Analogy:** Land in Thailand with valid visa but 14-day quarantine. Can't access anything yet
- **Finance:** Health insurance: 30 days general, 120 days critical illness. Sick on Day 29 = not covered
- **Verdict:** "Check waiting periods. For critical illness, often 120 days. Plan accordingly."

---

### **CATEGORY C: BEHAVIORAL & PSYCHOLOGICAL (#018-#022)**

---

#### **#018: The Birthday Price Tag** (Age-Banding)
- **Analogy:** Every product's price increases on YOUR birthday. Milk at 30 = 50 Baht. Milk at 60 = 200 Baht
- **Finance:** Health insurance at 35 = 30K/year. At 55 = 90K/year. At 65 = might not sell at all
- **Verdict:** "Get quoted today AND at age +5 and +10. See the birthday price tag."

---

#### **#019: The Automatic Investor** (DCA)
- **Analogy:** Rice cooker. Put in rice and water, press button, walk away. Doesn't panic when water boils
- **Finance:** DCA = rice cooker. 10K/month, automatic. Buys more when low, less when high. No emotion
- **Verdict:** "Set up automatic monthly investment. Remove yourself from the kitchen."

---

#### **#020: The Oxygen Tank** (Emergency Fund)
- **Analogy:** Scuba diver measures tank in minutes of survival, not liters. "20 minutes of air" means everything
- **Finance:** Emergency fund isn't 300K Baht—it's "6 months of oxygen." 50K/month expenses = 6 months survival
- **Verdict:** "Calculate monthly burn rate. Divide emergency fund by that. Less than 6 months = half-empty tank."

---

#### **#021: The Two Buckets** (Debt vs Investment)
- **Analogy:** One hose, two buckets. One has hole (debt leaking interest). One is solid (investment growing slowly)
- **Finance:** Credit card = 18% hole. Investment = 7% solid bucket. Fill leaking bucket first
- **Verdict:** "List debts by interest rate. Any above 8%? Plug that hole before investing."

---

#### **#022: The Hospital Maze** (Claims Process)
- **Analogy:** Golden ticket to theme park. But must navigate maze: forms, stamps, lines. Many give up before riding
- **Finance:** Insurance policy = golden ticket. Claims = maze. Wrong form = back to start
- **Verdict:** "Learn the maze before you need it. Ask agent: 'Walk me through a claim step-by-step.'"

---

### **CATEGORY D: LONG-TERM PLANNING (#023-#025)**

---

#### **#023: The House Before the Flood** (Estate Planning)
- **Analogy:** Sandbags have infinite value day before flood. Day after = zero
- **Finance:** Will costs 10K and 2 hours today. After you're gone = millions in fees, years in court, family conflict
- **Verdict:** "Is your will written? If not, schedule this month. The flood doesn't send calendar invites."

---

#### **#024: The Currency Seesaw** (FX Risk)
- **Analogy:** Seesaw: you on THB end, investment on USD end. Dollar rises = heavier. Dollar falls = lighter
- **Finance:** US fund gained 10% in USD. Baht strengthened 8%. Real gain = 2%
- **Verdict:** "Track TWO numbers: fund return AND currency movement. Real return is both."

---

#### **#025: The Snowball vs Avalanche** (Compounding)
- **Analogy:** Snowball grows slowly then explodes. Avalanche is dramatic but ends quickly
- **Finance:** At 7%, money doubles in 10 years. Year 0-10: 1M→2M. Year 20-30: 4M→8M. Same time, 4x more added
- **Verdict:** "Start the snowball TODAY. First 10 years slow. Next 10 magic. Last 10 life-changing."
